HRIS & Payroll Transformation
Turned a recurring payroll fire into a zero-touch automated system — and rebuilt performance management alongside it

The Situation

The company was running payroll multiple times per month due to persistent manual errors — wrong deductions, missed overtime, attendance mismatches. Finance was spending hours on reruns. Every payroll cycle was a crisis, not an operation.

What I Did

I led the full HRIS implementation end-to-end — vendor evaluation, data migration, workflow configuration and team training. I mapped every failure point in the old process and eliminated them: automated attendance syncing, rule-based payroll calculation, and a mandatory pre-run audit check. I simultaneously used the platform to automate the full performance management cycle — replacing the spreadsheets managers had been struggling with.

Resource Cost & Margin Control
Turned idle headcount from a quarterly surprise into a managed metric — held at 3–4% of MoM revenue

The Situation

In a consulting and tech firm, bench cost is a direct margin leak. No structure around allocation — no billing tracking, no idle time ceiling, no accountability. Resources sat between projects fully salaried, unbilled. Leadership only saw it in the P&L after the damage was done.

What I Did

I designed a floater cost framework from scratch — defining bench time, setting a target band (3–4% of MoM revenue), and building a weekly visibility report for leadership. I worked with project leads on faster reallocation, shadow staffing, and internal upskilling. Bench cost became a managed line item, not a surprise.

APAC HR Build-Out
Built a full HR function from zero across 3 countries — policies, compliance, onboarding and performance in one cycle

The Situation

The company was operating across multiple APAC markets with no formal HR infrastructure. Contracts were inconsistent, no local policy frameworks, no onboarding, no performance cycle, and growing compliance exposure across three labour jurisdictions — while headcount was scaling fast.

What I Did

Extended coverage to 5 additional APAC markets without adding headcount via a centralised compliance tracker teams could self-serve.

Culture & Performance Design
Designed a performance system and culture framework that reduced attrition and gave managers real accountability tools

The Situation

There was no structured performance process. Feedback was ad hoc, top performers had no growth path, underperformance went unaddressed. Culture was unintentional — and attrition was the result.

What I Did

Built a performance management system integrated into the HRIS — structured review cycles, goal-setting frameworks, and manager calibration sessions. Alongside this, I designed a culture framework: defining values, embedding them into onboarding and recognition, and tracking progress via engagement surveys. HR shifted from reactive to proactive — people problems identified before they became attrition.
