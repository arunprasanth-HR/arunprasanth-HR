The Situation

The company was running payroll multiple times a month due to persistent errors in manual processing — wrong deductions, missed overtime, attendance data mismatches. Finance was spending hours on reruns. HR was reactive, not strategic. Every payroll cycle was a crisis.

What I Did

I led the full HRIS implementation — from vendor selection and data migration to workflow design and team training. I mapped every failure point in the old process and configured the system to eliminate them. Automated attendance syncing, rule-based payroll calculation, and a pre-run audit check were built in from day one. I also used the same HRIS to automate the performance management cycle — removing the manual tracking spreadsheets that managers had been maintaining separately.

Resource Cost & Margin Control
Built a resource allocation framework that turned idle cost into a managed financial metric
3–4%
Floater cost / MoM revenue
Visible
Bench cost to leadership

The Situation

In a consulting and tech environment, resources sitting between projects are a direct drain on margin. There was no system to track idle time, no billing structure, and no ceiling on how long someone could be unallocated. By the time leadership noticed, it was already in the P&L.

What I Did

I designed a floater cost framework — defining what counts as bench time, setting an acceptable threshold (3–4% of MoM revenue), and building a weekly visibility dashboard for leadership. I worked with project leads to reduce idle gaps through internal upskilling projects, shadow staffing, and faster reallocation. This made bench cost a managed line item, not a surprise.
